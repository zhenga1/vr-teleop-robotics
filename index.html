Intuitive VR Teleoperation for High-Fidelity Robot Demonstrations

We collect real-world manipulation trajectories on a UR7e using Meta Quest 3 controllers, store demonstrations in readable logs, and replay motion with conditional execution based on perception.

Platform: UR7e
Interface: Meta Quest 3 (SteamVR/ALVR)
Stack: ROS 2 + MoveIt
Outputs: .txt logs (+ dataset placeholders)
Project goal
Reliable data collection for robot learning
Demonstrations are recorded during teleoperation and replayed for training and evaluation.
Recording rate
2 Hz sampling (0.5 s)
Aligned recording and replay for smoother execution (placeholder values‚Äîupdate if changed).
Conditional execution
Color-based selection ‚Üí job queue ‚Üí replay
Vision module selects which trajectory to execute based on sensed scene state.

Overview

what & why

Why data collection matters

Modern robot learning methods are data-hungry‚Äîpolicy quality depends strongly on the diversity and fidelity of real-world demonstrations.

Our contribution

An intuitive VR teleoperation workflow using Meta Quest 3 controllers to collect UR7e manipulation trajectories, stored as readable logs for replay and dataset building.

Key capabilities

(1) VR-based 6-DoF control, (2) high-fidelity recording & storage, (3) trajectory replay on hardware, (4) vision-conditioned selection/sorting.
